About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the School of Electrical, Computer and Energy Engineering at Arizona State University, working under the supervision of Dr. Ying-Cheng Lai. My research focuses on quantum reservoir computing and artificial intelligence for laser systems.

I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at ASU in 2026, with research emphasis on quantum physics and machine learning. I received the Dean's Dissertation Award for my contributions. My academic background includes a M.S. in Theoretical Physics from Lanzhou University and a B.S. in Theoretical Physics.

My research interests span quantum computing, machine learning applications in complex systems, entanglement engineering, and the intersection of physics and artificial intelligence. I am passionate about leveraging computational methods to solve challenging problems in quantum mechanics and nonlinear dynamics.
